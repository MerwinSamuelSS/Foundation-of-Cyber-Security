## 1 - Security and Risk Management
Security risk management is the systematic process of identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks that could negatively impact an organization's assets and operations. Focused on defining security goals and objectives, risk mitigation, compliance, business continuity and legal regulation.

## Defining security goals and objectives 
Goal and objective of the organization is to reduce risk of critical assests and data like PII (Personally identifiable information)

## Risk mitagation 
Risk mitigation is the process of having the right procedures and rules in place to quickly reduce the impact of a risck like breach.

## Compliance:
Compliance is the promary method used to develop an organization's internal security policies, regulatory requirements and independent standard.

## Business continuity 
An organization's ability to maintain their everyday productivity by establishing risk disaster recovery plans. 

## Laws (Legal regulation)
Laws related to security and risk management are differenet in different countries, following the rules and regulation of the specific country for ethical behavior to minimize negligence, abuse or fraud.

## Key Components of Security Risk Management

## Identification of Risks:
This involves recognizing potential threats to an organization, which can include cyber threats, physical security breaches, insider threats, and other vulnerabilities that could lead to harm or loss.

## Assessment and Analysis: 
Once risks are identified, they are analyzed to understand their nature, likelihood, and potential impact on the organization. This helps prioritize risks based on their severity and the resources required to address them.

## Mitigation Strategies:
After assessing risks, organizations develop and implement strategies to minimize or eliminate them. This can include technological solutions (like firewalls and encryption), organizational policies (such as access controls), and physical security measures.

## Continuous Monitoring:
Security risk management is an ongoing process that requires regular monitoring and review of the risk environment. This ensures that new risks are identified and that existing controls remain effective against evolving threats.

## Integration with Organizational Goals: 
Effective security risk management aligns with the overall objectives of the organization, ensuring that security practices are integrated into all levels of planning and operations. This helps protect assets, preserve stakeholder trust, and achieve strategic objectives.

## 3 - Security Architecture and Engineering
Focused on optimizing data security by ensuring effective tools, systems and processes are in place to protect an organization assets and data.
Core concept of Security architecture and engineering is shared responsibility

## Shared Responsibility 
The shared responsibility model in security architecture and engineering is a framework that defines the division of security responsibilities between cloud service providers (CSPs) and their customers. CSPs manage the security of the cloud infrastructure itself, while customers must secure their data, applications, and configurations. This model is essential for cloud adoption, as it helps organizations understand their roles and responsibilities in maintaining security. It is not a handoff to avoid accountability; it is a governance, engineering, and operational discipline that maps ownership to capabilities, controls, and telemetry.

## Shared Responsibility in Security Architecture and Engineering in AWS

The Shared Responsibility Model in AWS security architecture and engineering is a fundamental concept that defines the division of security responsibilities between AWS and its customers. AWS is responsible for the security of the cloud, which includes the physical security of data centers, the infrastructure that runs AWS services, and the global infrastructure that supports AWS regions and availability zones. Customers are responsible for security in the cloud, which involves securing their data, applications, operating systems, network configurations, and access management. This model ensures that AWS manages the underlying infrastructure, while customers have control over their data and how they configure their cloud environment.
